Academic Projects

Love Our Earth Sustainability Series

At Wallis Annenberg GenSpace, I proposed, conceptualized, and taught a three-class series titled Love Our Earth. This class was designed to empower GenSpace members in learning more about why being a climate-friendly ally is important in navigating the specific impact our world’s changes have on older adults. I also designed the course with the intent of discussing how we can strengthen our eco-friendly habits, how we can protect themselves in the face of climate change, and how we can connect with nature in different ways. Covering sustainable lifestyles, eco-friendly habits, and the intersection of climate change and aging, Love Our Earth used original presentations, discussions, and interactive activities to engage members in fostering their connections with sustainability.

Below you can find the Love Our Earth Sustainability Series Impact Report, which provides a recap of the course, statistics from feedback forms, and future sustainability programming ideas for Wallis Annenberg GenSpace. This report is intended to provide senior leadership a greater insight into intern programs.

Reimagining the “Senior Center” as a Public Health Initiative: A Wallis Annenberg GenSpace Case Study

For my Advanced Writing course, my professor tasked our class with writing a focused research argument that utilizes both primary and secondary research on one specific topic of choice. Given my interests in community centers to aid in aging in place, I chose to write about my current place of employment, Wallis Annenberg GenSpace, and why we should begin to think of senior centers as sources to execute public health initiatives.

I conducted 10 interviews with staff and members as primary research, which builds the foundation of the paper. I also composed an annotated bibliography for this paper.

Sexual Rights for Social Change Academy

During my time as a fellow for the USC Institute on Inequalities in Global Health's 2024 Sexual Rights for Social Change Academy, I created 12 short-form videos on social media about reproductive justice, comprehensive sex education, censorship, and HIV stigma. By conducting independent research, storyboarding, and utilizing popular filming techniques, I was able to make space for discussions on taboo topics in a fun, engaging way. For my capstone project, I presented a mock campaign titled, “Expanding Medicaid Access in Florida for Sexual Health Services,” that featured 1 short-form video and persuasive speech. I wanted to make my capstone about health insurance, because of my gerontology background. Florida is one of 10 states that has not yet expanded Medicaid, so this topic resonates with me, as I firmly believe my peers should have access to these critical services.
